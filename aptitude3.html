Chain Rule

The chain rule is a fundamental rule in calculus that allows for the computation of the derivative of a composite function.The chain rule allows us to handle situations where the dependent variable of a function is affected by other intermediate variables.

Height and Distance

Volume and Surface area

Volume refers to the amount of space occupied by a three-dimensional object.Cubic units: cubic meters (m³), cubic centimeters (cm³), or liters (L).
Surface area refers to the total area of the outer surface of a three-dimensional object. Square units:square meters (m²), square centimeters (cm²), or square feet (ft²).
Formulas:

  • Volume: V = a³
  • Surface Area: A = 6a²
  • Volume: V = lwh
  • Surface Area: A = 2lw + 2lh + 2wh
  • Volume: V = (4/3)πr³
  • Surface Area: A = 4πr²
  • Volume: V = πr²h
  • Surface Area: A = 2πrh + 2πr²
