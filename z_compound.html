Compound Interest- General Questions

1.The difference between simple and compound interests compounded annually on a certain sum of money for 2 years at 4% per annum is Re. 1. The sum (in Rs.) is?

A.625

B.630

C.640

D.650

Option A

Let the sum be Rs. x. Then,
C.I. = x [1 + 4/100]2 - x = 676/625 x - x = 51/625 x.
S.I. = x x 4 x 2 /100 = 2/25x .
51/625x - 2/25x = 1
x = 625.

2.The compound interest on Rs. 30,000 at 7% per annum is Rs. 4347. The period (in years) is ?

A.2 cm

B.2*1/2m

C.3m

D.4cm

Option A

Amount = Rs. (30000 + 4347) = Rs. 34347.
Let the time be n years.
Then, 30000 [1 + 7 /100]n = 34347
[107 /100] n = 34347/30000 = 11449/10000 =[107/100]2
n = 2 years.

3.What will be the compound interest on a sum of Rs. 25,000 after 3 years at the rate of 12 p.c.p.a.?

A. Rs. 9000.30

B.Rs. 9720

C.Rs. 10123.20

D. Rs. 10483.20

Option C

Amount = Rs. 25000 x [ 1 + 12/100]3
= Rs. 25000 x 28/25 x 28/25 x 28/25 = Rs. 35123.20
C.I. = Rs. (35123.20 - 25000) = Rs. 10123.20

5. The difference between simple interest and compound on Rs. 1200 for one year at 10% per annum reckoned half-yearly is?

A. Rs. 2.50

B. Rs. 3

C.Rs. 3.75

D.Rs. 4

Option B

S.I. = Rs[1200 x 10 x 1 /100] = Rs. 120.
C.I. = Rs.[1200x[1+5/100]2-1200 ] = Rs. 123.
Difference = Rs. (123 - 120) = Rs. 3.
